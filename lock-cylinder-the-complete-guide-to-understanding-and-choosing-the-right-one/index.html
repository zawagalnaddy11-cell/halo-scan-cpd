What Is a Lock Cylinder?

A lock cylinder—also known as the key cylinder—is the central mechanism where the key is inserted to operate the lock. It contains several small pins and springs that align perfectly when the correct key is used, allowing the plug to turn and unlock the door.

Lock cylinders are found in a wide range of locking systems, including deadbolts, mortise locks, padlocks, and door handles. They are valued for their flexibility, as they can be replaced or rekeyed without changing the entire lock.

How Does a Lock Cylinder Work?

The lock cylinder works using a pin tumbler mechanism. Inside the cylinder, there are a set of spring-loaded pins divided into upper and lower halves. When an incorrect key is inserted, the pins are misaligned, preventing the plug from turning. However, when the right key is used, the pins align along the shear line, allowing smooth rotation and unlocking of the door.

This system is simple yet highly effective, and it remains the standard in most residential and commercial locks today.
